Assessment

A simplified guide assessing a building.

General House Assessment

  • Consider the Macro surrounding Landscape. Whether located on a hill, in a valley, water courses and railways/roads etc.
  • Consider the close proximity environment. Nearby Trees, houses, telegraph poles, general features that you can see from your front door.

Room Assessment

A simplified guide to doing your own assessment of a room.

Nb. A whole house or alternative building has other factors to consider, but is fundamentally the same. eg. A room whilst being feed with its own directional energy is sourced from the main energy initially feeding the building.

This repeats some of information in the Theory Section, however puts it in sequential context.

  • Sketch a simple plan of the room including doors, windows, wall projections, chimney breasts and fixed furniture (fitted cupboards etc).
  • Draw an overlay grid, dividing the room into 8 perimeter spaces, 9 squares in total including the middle space. If the room is shaped unconventionally see the footnote on this page.
  • Stand at the door entrance and take a magnetic compass reading of the flow of Qi(energy) into the room.
  • Note the main compass point and element affecting the room. eg. If coming from South the element would be Fire.
  • Label the perimeter squares with the number corresponding to the Earthly Bagua or 'Later Heavenly Sequence'. eg. If the entrance door is facing (outwards) South and in the middle of the wall then the square would be #8.
  • Note the door position. (left, right, center). This determined whether it is a Yin, Yang or Lung room. (passive, active or neutral)
  • Note the window positions and the energy exchanges there affecting the room.
  • Using the Heavenly Bagua or 'Former Heavenly Sequence' portent maps for the main orientation (element) of the room label each square with appropriate letter. This gives us the fortune based on energy exchange of each space.

Room Reading

  • The room type (Yin, Yang or neutral) gives an idea as to whether the room is best suited for activity or rest.
    A Yang room: best for activity.
