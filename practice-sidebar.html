Feng Shui - General

The Taoist Art of Feng Shui is used to determine the best siting and planning of a building. It includes assessing a buildings location in the landscape, room layouts and detailed furnishing character and postitioning.
Origionaly it was primarily used for Palace design but subsequently became commonly adopted. Nowadays it is a must before moving or setting up businesses in large parts of Asia. (from Banks to homes).

It is based on Taoist knowledge of universal energy exchanges which were derived from the writings of Lao Tzu (the "Tao Te Ching" and "Hua Hua Ching") and the "I Ching" (the classic "book of change"). Other practices were absorbed over time.
The same principles apply to all Taoist practices including Tai Qi Chuan, Qi Gong, Acupuncture and Internal Alchemy.

The aim is to balance all the energies in the environment to suit the occupants. Divination/assessment is done by observing energy exchanges (planetary movement, the larger landscape/topography/orientation, local immediate landscape and small internal spaces). Horoscopes of individuals are also calculated to refine suitability. Planning or alterations, internal design and furnishing suggestions are then made based on the assessment.

Feng Shui is about balancing Qi (energy) so residents can live in harmony with the environment.
From receiving a good flow of energy from the larger topographical surroundings, to internal flows of energy circulating to all rooms, to the fine positioning of objects.
