Reading List

Beware! Information (books) are dangerous.
( never accept anything before thinking it through, and even then continually question ).

The two main texts of Lao Tzu really contain the basic information.
TAO TE CHING, Lao Tsu (Shambhala)
HUA HUA CHING Lao Tzu (Shambhala)
( there are other translations available, which vary, obviously there is always interpretation. Ideally it is worth reading a few and trying to determine, what you believe the gist is. However the Shambhala ones seem the best to me. )

Feng Shui specific books, I find it difficult to recommend any, there is common ground across the more traditional ones. The "new age", "shamanic" fusion ones seem to thrive on "balony", intention, affermations, symbology with a splash of "hocus pocus". This might on a trivial level have some kind of placebo effect, but is unsustainable for any growth. As with all challenges, ones the illusion is dissipated, it becomes familiar and you can move on without believing it's magic. There has been a tendancy to present "Feng Shui" as "coffee table" design chic. As with most things, it's best to avoid the "froth and glitter" and seek out the substance. Taken on a trivial "design aid" level, it may have some positive benifits. But...I comment too much*******(simplify)
