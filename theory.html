Preamble

In order to grasp Feng Shui one needs to have a basic understanding of Taoist philosophy and theory. The next section attempts to do this.

Taoism is a way of being or path not a religion (see the quote opposite).
It is thought to originate from the actions and writings (apparently under duress) of Lao Tzu.
Myth has it that Lao Tzu lived 700 years.

The Taoist text the "Tao Te Ching" written by Lao Tzu, sometime between 600 BC and 400 BC, is poetic in style and concise in information. His later work, the "Hua Hua Ching", contains more specific information on energetic interactions.
Lao Tzu realised that everything was connected, the planets, the elements and all the living things. He deduced that there were common elemental forces and attributes that link everything in the cosmos.

By connecting to these forces through Meditation and Taoist practices (such as Qi Gong, Tai Qi Chuan & Internal Alchemy) one is able to increase physical and subtle energy levels and build a strong platform for spiritual development.
It is like connecting the positive (heaven) and negative (earth) electric leads to give power.

Section One

Geomancy is "Earth Divination" involving the reading of energetic interactions on earth and how they affect us. Most ancient cultures (Aboriginal, African, American Indian, Druid, Celtic, Chinese, Egyptian, Indian, Nordic, Mayan and the Renaissance period, etc.) had/have their own systems of explanation.

Feng Shui, the Chinese Taoist form of Geomancy, provides the most comprehensive and well documented type.


Section Two

The theory underlying Feng Shui is the same for all Taoist practices. (including; Martial arts (Tai Qi Chuan, Qi Gong, etc.), Medicine (Acupuncture, Herbal remedies etc.) and Internal Alchemy).

Energy (called "Qi") its creation, varied qualities and interactions is key.

There are a number of Schools of Feng Shui: The Form School, The Compass School, The Black Sect School, The Flying Star School & The Classical School.

Section Three

Feng is Qi (energy) that flows above the ground and occupies every space there is, it flows as wind, and is fast moving. It's characteristics are dependent upon the direction in which it flows and the environment it occupies. It can be partially described as electromagnetically charged particles in the atmosphere.

Shui is Qi (energy) that is contained within the earth and is inherent in the structure of a building (mass), it is slow moving. Shui can be experienced as a cool blue energy, hence being called water, it is essentially gravitational force.

The practice of Feng Shui aims to balance these energies to benefit the occupant/s. It is a system of assessing the quality of energies at play and calculating how they interact.
