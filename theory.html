The 8 Directions

The Chinese discovered that Qi (energy) had differing characteristics depending upon the direction of movement. They derived a system of reading these energetic interactions using the main Elements.
Essentially a flavor of the main 5 elements energy by directions.
Eg. Imagine: A field of particles moving in one direction has certain qualities, moving in another direction is has a different quality. It makes sense in that the earth magnetism is, somewhat, constant and any directional change will produce a different outcome.

A Compass (Lo Pan) is used to obtain information on orientation and qualities of energy.
It has a normal magnetic compass at its center with radiating additional markings indicating the main 8 directions, the former and later heavenly sequences, further elemental subdivisions (down to 2 degress), portents (fortune/misfortune ) of spaces, Water Dragon information and more.

The Baguas

Bagua is the Chinese for 8 directions.
The 8 main directions are illustrated opposite. They are general divisions of the main directional energies.

The 8 directions each have characteristics of the 5 elements. With 3 variants. | N=Water | NE=small Earth | E=Wood | SE=small Wood | S=Fire | SW=Earth | W=Metal | NW=small Metal |
SW and NE whilst both being of the earth element differed markedly in quality. So to with W & NW (metal) and E & SE (wood).

NB.The eight main directions are the most commonly used, however there are further subdivisions (2 degree divisions) which can give a more detailed divination - for specific work (desk) and furniture positioning and the calculation of the "Water Dragon" (orientation of external water flows in relation to a building).

Heavenly Bagua. The inner Trigrams relate to the "Former Heavenly Sequence". A system derived to explain the energy of free flowing Feng.

Earthly Bagua. The outer Trigrams relate to the "Later Heavenly Sequence". A system derived to explain the exchange of energy within a building/space. Essentially the earth energy (Shui) component inherent in the structure or gravitational effect .

The Heavenly Bagua is moveable and the Earthly Bagua is fixed. Correctly combining and reading these two Bagua's gives the portent of a particular space.

NB. The Chinese always show fire (south) at the top because it relates to the heavens(S, fire, yang) above and the earth(N, water, yin) below.

The 5 Element Cycle

The five element cycle is the key to understanding energy exchange. There are regular and abnormal cycles which are described below.

Normal and regular (generative) cycle
The creative positive interaction of energies.
Fire feeds earth ( ash / nutrients ).
Earth feeds metal ( minerals ).
Metal feeds water ( metal sweats ).
Water feeds wood.
Wood feeds fire.
Earth is at the centre, with it's harmonising influence.

Abnormal (control) cycle
The controling and/or destructive interaction of energies
