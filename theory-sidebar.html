Introduction

The theory behind Feng Shui is the same for all Taoist practices including Tai Qi, Qi Gong, Acupuncture and internal Alchemy.
Various energies were identified and a system of classification and calculating their interactions was developed.

    Energy types:
  • Wu Qi : Original Source energy >
  • Tai Qi : Balanced fusion of Yin & Yang energies > (splits into)
  • Yin : Female energy, -'ve charge &
  • Yang : Male energy, +'ve charge. >
  • The 5 Elements : Five general energy types >
  • The 8 Directions : Eight directional energies >
  • The 64 Hexagram's : 8x8 interactions, more specific types.
The Basic premise is;
There was/is an original source energy, 'Wu Qi', from which the cosmos was born and continually recreates. This is unlike the energy present in the physical Universe we know of, it is a unity (one). ie. Has no +'ve nor -'ve charge.

'Wu Qi' energy transforms from a unity (one) to binary (two) when it becomes physical. It splits into Yin (female, -'ve charge) and Yang (male, +'ve charge) energies. All things in the physical Universe are consist of varying, individually specific, proportions of Yin & Yang. The proportion determines character, properties and interaction.

Five main energy types, with generally similar qualities, were identified as 'The Five Elements'. In Scientific language they can be thought of as the four fundamental forces: Gravity, Electromagnetism, Strong Nuclear and Weak Nuclear. The Taoists identified and included a fifth unifying force called 'Earth'.

The use of the '5 Elements' is real and symbolic. eg. The Element Water is the main energy found in water, but also is the main element in a approximately a fifth of everything (the planet Mercury and the Kidney organs for instance)
Each Element describes its own inherent qualities and how it interacts with the others. eg. Wood feeds Fire (this would be a 'creative' exchange), whereas, Water douses Fire (this would be a controlling/destructive exchange).
Proportion is obviously a factor. eg. one cup of water won't extinguish a bonfire.

The two '5 Element Cycles' express generative and controlling exchanges. Further detail can be calculated using other methods, however still based on the Elements.

Energy has different qualities based on direction (eg. energy coming from the south has the property of Fire) and so a system called The 8 Directions (Bagua) was devised to describe energy types based on Compass orientation. [same 5 Elements with three additional distinct sub-flavors. eg. 3 additional directional energies were included, small Earth, small Wood & small Metal.]

More Detailed Explanation

Wu Qi

WuQi is the original source from which the cosmos was and is born/reborn. It is a unity, a single thing, not of the physical cosmos. /* scientists might call it dark matter, the god particle, or indeed anti-matter. However one describes it, if at all right, it is "nothingness" */

Yin & Yang

Yin and Yang lie at the core of all Traditional Chinese explanations of life on earth. ( yang= solid line, yin=broken line ) Everything in the Spiritual realm is a unity. Everything on the physical plane is a duality.

Yin and Yang are the two primary forces, they are opposites that compliment. Yang is the male principle or +'ve charge and Yin is the female principle or -'ve charge. The proportion, quality and interactions of these two forces give rise to all varied physical manifestations. Yin and Yang balanced and in harmony is called 'Tai Qi'. The proportion of yin and yang in perfect (imperfect) harmony is 1: 1.61803. [ Which is also the proportion of 'Phi' and the 'Golden Section' ]
